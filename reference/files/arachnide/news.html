18. December 2000  -  Development paused
knox
    Hi, Arachnide development has currently paused for some weeks out of following reasons:
    • a) I'm spending really a lot too much time infront of a screen.
    • b) Right now I've another very interesting project stealing my attention.

    Well, it doesn't mean I gave up, or it's to an end. I know myself a little after two dozends of years... Sometimes suddendly getting some impulse from limbo, giving development another boost.

    Well after all I've released now a binary version! I planned to have 0.1 playable at least and be rather stable, however didn't come that far. However I feel obliged to release a binary, before taking off to holidays.

    Arachnide 0.1 has the world-drawing-engine implemented.

    Download Arachnide 0.1 at the files page, sit back and enjoy the speed :o)

    It easily beats OSI client a far way on frame-rate, and reload speed.
    (Well walking has still a lot of jerks, and sometimes it crashes, sorry 'bout that...)
    And if you have sometime left, also checkout out the easter-bunnies...

    And so the saga continues...
    Arachnide is the successor of UMelange, my UO client written in java, which is now available on the history page.

    This time it's all written in C++ using SDL as basic layer, and is thus rather platform independant. However my main development platform is windows.

    Messageboard, CVS/Source and all that cute things are available at "Arachnide's homestead" at SourceForge
